SFM: Food Security Application

Modeling Oil Palm Seedling Biomass Accumulation

Interactive demonstration of Derivatives $f'(x)$ in Malaysian Agriculture

The Bioscience Problem

In sustainable food security, maximizing early-stage crop vigor reduces nursery mortality and ensures robust field yields. This model simulates the vegetative biomass growth of a high-yield Malaysian DxP Rosgen oil palm clone seedling over its first 12 months in a nursery.

Parameters adapted realistically from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) nursery guideline frameworks and biological logistic trends.

Mathematical Modeling

Biomass Function f(x)
f(x) = 500 / (1 + e-0.5(x - 6))

Yields the total cumulative biomass in grams at month x.

The Derivative f'(x) (Growth Rate)
f'(x) = [250 · e-0.5(x - 6)] / (1 + e-0.5(x - 6))2

Calculates the instantaneous growth rate (grams/month) at exact time x.
