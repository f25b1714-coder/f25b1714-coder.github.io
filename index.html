Applying Derivatives to Malaysian Crop Yields

Optimizing Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) harvesting in Elaeis guineensis (Oil Palm) using rate of change analysis.

The Food Security Problem

In Malaysia, maximizing palm oil production per hectare is vital for global food security and land-use efficiency. Oil palm Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) follow a non-linear biological growth curve. Harvesting too early results in underdeveloped fruit with low oil content; harvesting too late leads to over-ripe fruit where oil degrades into free fatty acids.

💡 The Calculus Solution: By analyzing the first derivative $f'(x)$ of the biomass function, agronomists can identify the precise inflection point where the growth rate peaks, signaling the ideal biological window for yield forecasting.

Data Reference Model

Based on growth dynamics parameters from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) and standard sigmoidal biological yield frameworks found in MPOB TT No. 640 (Oil Palm Yield Performance Reports).

Realistic parameters: Max Bunch Weight ($L$) = 25kg, Growth rate ($k$) = 0.35/week.

Biomass Growth Function

$$f(x) = \frac{25}{1 + e^{-0.35(x - 12)}}$$

Predicts the weight of the FFB ($f(x)$ in kg) at week $x$ of fruit set.

Growth Rate Function (Derivative)

$$f'(x) = \frac{8.75 \cdot e^{-0.35(x - 12)}}{(1 + e^{-0.35(x - 12)})^2}$$

Calculates the instantaneous rate of weight gain ($f'(x)$ in kg/week) at exactly week $x$.
