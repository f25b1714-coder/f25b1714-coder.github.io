Predicting Oil Palm FFB Growth via Derivatives

An interactive bioscience application for SFM: Food Security undergraduates.

The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysia, optimizing the harvest timing of Oil Palm Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) is vital for maximizing crude palm oil yield and securing the supply chain. As the fruit develops over a standard 180-day micro-cycle, its biomass accumulation follows a sigmoidal (S-shaped) logistic curve.

By applying the first derivative $f'(x)$, agronomists can pinpoint the exact day where the accumulation rate peaks (the inflection point). Harvesting too early cuts potential yield; harvesting too late allows fatty acids to degrade oil quality.

Mathematical Modeling

Based on generalized growth curves adapted from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Yield Performance Reports.

Biomass Function $f(x)$:
$f(x) = \frac{5}{1 + e^{-0.05(x - 90)}}$
Growth Rate Derivative $f'(x)$:
$f'(x) = \frac{0.25 \cdot e^{-0.05(x - 90)}}{(1 + e^{-0.05(x - 90)})^2}$

*Where $x$ = Days since fruit set, $f(x)$ = FFB Weight (kg), and $f'(x)$ = Growth rate (kg/day).
